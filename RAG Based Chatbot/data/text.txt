Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an Indian scientist and visionary known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in developing ballistic missiles and India's nuclear program, serving as the 11th President (2002-2007), and inspiring millions with his simplicity, dedication to science, and emphasis on empowering youth and achieving developed nation status by 2020. Born in Rameswaram, he rose from humble beginnings to lead India's space and defense research, becoming a beloved "People's President" known for his inspirational books and advocacy for education. 

Bhagat Singh (1907-1931) was a prominent Indian anti-colonial revolutionary, a key figure in India's independence struggle, known for his courage, socialist ideals, and willingness to sacrifice for his country, becoming a national hero and martyr after his execution by the British at age 23 for his role in assassinating a police officer and bombing the Central Legislative Assembly to protest repressive laws. 

Subhash Chandra Bose (1897-1945) was a charismatic Indian nationalist and freedom fighter known as "Netaji" (Respected Leader), famous for his militant approach to India's independence from British rule, famously rallying with Axis powers during World War II to form the Indian National Army (INA) and declaring, "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom". Born in Cuttack, he excelled academically, passed the ICS exam but resigned to join the independence movement, leading radical factions, and inspiring millions with his fierce patriotism before his mysterious death in a plane crash in 1945, which remains a subject of debate. 

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630-1680) was a legendary Indian warrior-king who founded the Maratha Empire in 17th-century India, known for his military genius, innovative guerrilla warfare (Ganimi Kava), and establishment of Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule) against the >>Mughal Empire and other Deccan Sultanates. He was a visionary ruler who implemented progressive administration, religious tolerance, a strong navy, and fair revenue systems, earning him reverence as the "Father of the Maratha Nation" and a symbol of Indian resistance and sovereignty. 

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (1891-1956), known as Babasaheb, was an Indian jurist, economist, politician, and social reformer, celebrated as the chief architect of India's Constitution and the first Law Minister. Born into a marginalized caste, he fought against untouchability, championed Dalit rights, and advocated for women's rights and labor welfare. A prolific scholar, he established educational institutions, influenced economic policies like the Reserve Bank of India, and converted to Buddhism with his followers in 1956 to escape caste discrimination, leaving a powerful legacy for social justice and equality.
